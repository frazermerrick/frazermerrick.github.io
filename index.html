About

I'm a composer and sound designer who enjoys working across a variety of mediums. From audio branding to short films, video games to art installations..

Since graduating with First Class Honours in Music Technology and Popular Music Studies from The University of Huddersfield, my work has been shown at film festivals around the world, including the renown Cannes Film Festival. I was also chosen for an internship with acclaimed composer/producer Nitin Sawhney. Here I worked on projects with the likes of the BBC, London Symphony Orchestra, The Royal Albert Hall, and many others.

Alongside composition, I'm also the co-founder of Teaboy Games. A small tech-startup developing our mobile game Fallen.

Bunny

Bunny by Adam Awni is a psychologic thriller, about when Stephen meets Abbie. A romantic meal at her house soon unleashes a painful memory of a disturbed past. Stephen's date rapidly unfolds into his worst nightmare.

Completing the sound design on this 30minute film was no small feat. From cleaning production audio to recording foley (including some fantastically gory sequences), from receiving the original mono OMF to delivering a 5.1 mix at the end. Interestingly a number of scenes featured unusable production audio (due to unfortunate background noise), which led to some scenes being re-created from scratch. It is a project I'm incredibly proud to have been a part of, and look forward to see how it fairs on the festival circuit.

UPDATE: Bunny has been selected for the renown Festival de Cannes, amongst others!

  • Director: Adam Awni
  • Date: November 2014
  • Role: Sound Designer

One Try Son

One Try Son is an experimental short from director Omer Kula. What I loved about this project was the amount of freedom Omer gave me to experiment. Creating tension from the extended silences broken with the dissonant phone rings. The art direction must also be celebrated, I can only imagine how long it took to clean up all that shredded paper!

  • Director: Omer Kula

Busking Turf Wars

A documentary film crew follow Steven Lockmoore, a passionate and dedicated busker as he shows us the ins and outs of life as a street musician. Steven isn't the most technically proficient musician but he has a love for what he does and a yearning to entertain. His livelihood is thrown into turmoil however with the arrival of a more gifted musician keen to take the spotlight from Steven. Paul Adrian is egotistical and slimy, but might be more deserving of the most prominent spot on the high street. Only a competitive 'busk off' can decide who will be the victor and who will have to count their change and walk away for good.

Mouthwash

Mouthwash (2014) from Pictures & Noise on Vimeo.

"Never underestimate a bottle of mouthwash."

Upon watching this film, sent to me by director Ross Birks, I knew exactly what style I wanted to achieve. With the experimental short based around the character chugging mouthwash, I envisaged the music to being built around samples of the human voice.

Taking influence from Cristobal Tapia de Veer's score for Utopia, I wrote the main theme with a quirky low male voice, and backing from a mellotron choir patch. The real eureka moment was when I found a sample library of the 90s hit toy 'Speak and Spell', which ended up forming the glitchy drums you hear throughout the score!

  • Director: Ross Birks
  • Date: December 2014
  • Role: Composer

Borko

Borko is set in a dystopian future, our protagonist is in a barren city landscape and becomes an unlikely keeper of a Spherical transportation device.

Since this short film in central London, but set in a sparse future, the decision was made to remove all location sound. Whilst the slow-motion opening needs no 'sound', the fight sequences had to be built from scratch to keep the environment as quiet as possible. Something that wouldve been impossible since it was filmed under a motorway!

  • Client: Carlos Buenaventura
  • Date: February 2015
  • Role: Composer and Sound Designer

Teaboy Games

In September 2014 I joined an project called GamesHub, a video games development program run by Steven Huckle (who worked on titles like The Sims and Tomb Raider). I joined the program with the assumption of making music for the various teams within the course, however 6 months later and I've co-founded Teaboy Games!

Our game Fallen is a reaction based mobile game, somewhere between Tetris and Peggle. Alongside producing the project, I also created all the graphics, animations and music. Its been a fantastic experience that has pushed me to learn a plethora of new skills and make some fantastic connections with the likes of Microsoft, Dlala Studios and Kumotion.

I'm incredibly excited about releasing our game and see this as the beginning of a completely new field for me.
