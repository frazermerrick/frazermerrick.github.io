About

I'm a composer and sound designer working across a variety of mediums, from feature films to audio branding, video games to art installations.

Since graduating with First Class Honours in Music Technology and Popular Music Studies from The University of Huddersfield, my work has been shown at film festivals around the world, including the renowned Cannes Film Festival. I was also chosen for an internship with acclaimed composer/producer Nitin Sawhney where I worked on projects like BBC - Human Planet and his score for Hitchcock's The Lodger with London Symphony Orchestra.

Alongside my composition work, in 2015 I founded Teaboy Games, a mobile-focused games development company. Our debut game, Fallen, was feautured by Apple in over 100 countries leading to over 50k downloads within a week. As Creative Director, I am the lead artist, composer/sound-designer and design and implement our marketing strategy .

Bunny

Bunny is a psychologic thriller about when Stephen meets Abbie. Their romantic meal soon unleashes a painful memory of a disturbed past, and Stephen's date rapidly unfolds into his worst nightmare.

Completing the sound design on this 30 minute film was no small feat. From cleaning production audio to recording foley (including some fantastically gory sequences) and delivering a 5.1 mix at the end. The biggest challenge was when we discovered a number of scenes featured unusable production audio (due to unfortunate background noise), which led to these being re-created from scratch.

Bunny has since gone on to be selected for the renown Festival de Cannes, amongst many other film festivals!

  • Director: Adam Awni
  • Date: November 2014
  • Role: Sound Designer

Get In

Ed Button's night takes a strange turn when he is picked up by two gangsters, in this West Yorkshire mafia themed short.

Shot in the back of car, there was no space for a boom mic so all audio was captured from a camera mounted shot gun mic. This meant the biggest task was reducing any road noise outside the vechicle but then adding the background ambience back into the mix in a more controlled manner. Subtle sounds of rain, and passing cars with the doppler effect help sell the ambience much more than the noise captured by the camera mic.

  • Director: Ross Birks
  • Date: March 2015
  • Role: Sound Designer

Friday

A video posted by Aura Films (@aurafilmsuk) on Jul 20, 2016 at 11:41am PDT

FRIDAY was assembled in just 48hrs, with all my sound design taking place in an afternoon.

Theres nothing like an impending deadline to really get the creativity flowing - but I had a fantastic time bringing this POV short to life with diegetic sound effects.

The tap, radio, teaspoon, kiss, sirens, helicopter - so much of the work was really embellishing what was already there and changing the landscape from a generic parks and streets to world on the verge of a dystopian apocalypse.

  • Director: Steven Dorrington
  • Date: July 2015
  • Role: Sound Designer

Busking Turf Wars

A documentary film crew follows Steven Lockmoore, a passionate and dedicated busker, as he shows us the ins and outs of life as a street musician. Steven isn't the most technically proficient musician but he has a love for what he does and a yearning to entertain. His livelihood is thrown into turmoil however with the arrival of a more gifted musician keen to take the spotlight from Steven.

Since Busking Turf Wars is musical by nature my role was to underscore Steven's more contemplative moments, about the world and his path through it.

  • Director: Peter Trifunovic
  • Date: March 2015
  • Role: Composer

Mouthwash

Upon watching this film, sent to me by director Ross Birks, I knew exactly what style I wanted to achieve. With the experimental short based around the danger of chugging mouthwash, I envisaged the music to being built around samples of the human voice.

Taking influence from Cristobal Tapia de Veer's score for the Channel 4 series Utopia, I wrote the main theme with a pitched-down male voice sound, with backing from a mellotron choir patch. The real eureka moment was when I found a sample library of the 90s hit toy 'Speak and Spell', which ended up forming the glitchy drums you hear throughout the score.

  • Director: Ross Birks
  • Date: December 2014
  • Role: Composer
