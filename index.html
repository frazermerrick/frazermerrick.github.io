About

Composer, Sound Designer and all round noise maker - I’m a sound artist who works across films, video games and interactive art installations. Before graduating with a first-class honours in Music Technology (Huddersfield University, 2012) I worked for acclaimed composer and performer Nitin Sawhney on his score BBC Human Planet. Since, my work has been shown at film festivals around the world, including Cannes (Bunny, 2015), I've created a chest thumping sub-bass installation with recordings I made at Colchester Zoo (Elephant in The Room, 2016) and setup Teaboy Games, a games design studio focused on beautifully unique mobile games.

If you need audio for your artistic or commercial project, then I can help. I enjoy exploring sound and creating bespoke compositions for every comission, no matter how big or small. I strive to make every project my best piece of work.

Pendulum



  • Client: Teaboy Games
  • Date: June 2017
  • Role: Composer and Sound Designer

Pendulum is a minimal and mindful puzzler. The aim was to create a relaxing and hypnotizing atmosphere to match the minimal graphics and gameplay.

With the game split into chapters, I created a track (which looped) for each chapter and centered it around a different key and organic drone underneath to match the gameplay - depending on the powerups, visual style and gameplay. I experimented creating drones from crashing waves, bursts of white noise and recordings of rain - which all help add an organic and human feel to an otherwise electronic score.

Bloom

  • Director: Sian Fan
  • Date: March 2017
  • Role: Composer

Sian Fan is an emerging artist who works with movement, the body and technology to explore and expose what it means to exist, both physically and spiritually. Drawing on her background in contemporary dance, she creates choreography that fragments and abstracts the body, as an exploration of the complexity and profundity of existence.

This dramatic green screen aerial performance was an exciting challenge, as due to deadlines I began composing before seeing any footage. I drew sonic influence from the intensity of Manga films and Andrew Thomas Huang's SOLIPSIST

Bloom was comissioned by Random Acts and will premiere later in 2017.

Busking Turf Wars

  • Director: Peter Trifunovic
  • Date: March 2015
  • Role: Composer

A documentary film crew follows Steven Lockmoore, a passionate and dedicated busker, as he shows us the ins and outs of life as a street musician. Steven isn't the most technically proficient musician but he has a love for what he does and a yearning to entertain. His livelihood is thrown into turmoil however with the arrival of a more gifted musician keen to take the spotlight from Steven.

Since Busking Turf Wars is musical by nature my role was to underscore Steven's more contemplative moments, about the world and his path through it.

CLIP

  • Find Out More: www.clipsoundandmusic.org.uk
  • Date: May 2015
  • Role: Co-Founder

CLIP is a weekly meet-up up of young musicians with a keen interest in the experimental. Through various collaborations and workshops, CLIP explore the boundaries between sound and music, where the process of creation becomes as intriguing as the final result. We use improvisation and experiments as a way to improve confidence and increase musical knowledge.

I formed CLIP with fellow sound artist Simon Keep in 2015 and we've been running workshops and performances ever since. Our biggest project to date has been working with Colchester Zoo to create ‘A Song For Elephants’, using recordings of their inhabitants pitched down to sub 100hz - which you can hear above.

Perpetual Chimes

  • Comissioner: Circuit Cambridge
  • Date: June 2016
  • Role: Sound Designer

For me, sound is a form of escapism - so with this project I wanted to explore this theme further and continue my research into the possibilities of using physical objects to trigger midi signals.

Commissioned by Circuit Cambridge (a young persons art group at Wysing Art Gallery), I was invited me to run a MakeyMakey workshop at their forthcoming event ‘Unlock Digital: Cambridge’ and also invited to create a small installation piece to show alongside it - and perpetual chimes were born.

After failed attempts to customise a garden wind chime, this franken-chime was built from copper and stainless steel pipe. Is it strikes the central collumn it triggers ethereal synth sounds atop a background of rain recordings - which you hear over the nearby headphones. In the middle of a busy exhibition floor, I had created a peaceful and tranquil environment to lose yourself within.

Visit my blog to read more about the project and its construction phase.

Phantom

  • Comissioner: Firstsite
  • Date: November 2015
  • Role: Sound Designer

As part of Firstsite's 'Museums at Night' evening, they turned off the lights and invited their Young Art Kommunity (YAK) to fill the building with neon and interactive art.

Phantom was born from a want to experiment with MakeyMakey, a small circuit board which allows you to attach physical objects as buttons to a computer. Inside each cupboard I placed two items, a metal plate and another conductive ‘object’. When the user touched both they’d complete a circuit and the MakeyMakey would read this as a particular key stroke. Inside Scratch (a very simple coding program) I wrote a small program that upon each key stroke would playback a sound and project an image (both relative to the object), depending on which objects were being touched. To add an extra sense of playfulness I then put some mirrorballs on a spinning platter in front of the projector to give the ‘night sky’ effect, check out the video above.
